    School Overview

    Tabuk City National High School is a public educational institution located in Tabuk City, Kalinga . The school provides quality, fair, culture-based and complete basic education to produce graduates who are intellectually and technologically co ...mplete basic education to produce graduates who are intellectually and technologically competent.     TCNHS History

    In 1986, A Community High School Was Proposed By The Late Mr. Augusto Alejandrino, The Then-District Supervisor Of Northern Tabuk District. It Was A Public School By The Ministry Of Education Without Co... gressional Act or executive order of its opening. The permit to open was granted by the Division Office of Kalinga-Apayao, without the benefit of classrooms and budgetary outlay. The school had two sections and three teachers, namely Mrs. Dominica Villamor, Mrs. Lolita Castillo, and Mr. Desiderio Orprecio.

    In 1970, the Provincial Government of Kalinga-Apayao headed by Hon. Amado Almazan approved a resolution converting the newly established Community High School to Kalinga-Apayao Provincial School. With the increase of enrollees, the third and fourth-year curricula were opened. All available rooms of Tabuk Central School were utilized in order to solve the problems of accommodation.

    The Provincial Government and the Division Office proposed a school budget which was good for twenty-four teaching positions. However, upon the opening of classes for the school year 1970-1971, thirty-three teachers were employed. The hiring of more teachers which is not in accordance with the budget resulted in the delay in their salaries. Despite the sad experiences of these teachers, they never relaxed in their classroom activities in imparting knowledge to their students.

    In April 1971, the first graduation of the Kalinga-Apayao Provincial High School took place. There were 53 graduates composed of 27 boys and 26 girls. The graduation was so memorable and gratifying to note for many of the graduating students passed the qualifying government examinations for possible scholarships in college.

    Among them was Miss Karen Sarmiento who passed the examination. She is the first Valedictorian of Kalinga-Apayao Provincial High School.

    In 1973, Republic Act No. 6470 mandated the conversion of the Kalinga-Apayao Provincial High School to Tabuk National High School. In June 1973, Superintendent Epifanio T. Ramos appointed Mrs. Joaquina C. Falgui as the first Principal of the Tabuk National High School.

    On June 27, 1988, President Corazon C. Aquino, through Proclamation No. 227, awarded Lot 1461, PS-93 with an area of 32,233 square meters to the Tabuk National High School.

    Tabuk City National High School has been producing desirable graduates for the community and the larger society as a whole. The school is now headed by Mrs. Mildred Sadagan Cabay. Her predecessors are: Dr. Rodolfo C. Ballog, Mr. Augusto Alejandrino, Mrs. Juaquina C. Falgui and Mrs. Purificacion E. Domingo.

    Tabuk City National High School initiated LOVE DRIVE to frontliners yesterday to show its appreciation to their hard work in protecting the citizens of this community.

    Eight community competencies were validated as the Indigenous People’s (IP) Month Celebration concluded on October 15.

    Marieta Bas-ilen, Araling Panlipunan Head Teacher and the IP Education focal person, said that the eight community competencies were validated by tribal leaders. The tribal leaders were... accredited by the National Commission on Indigenous Peoples (NCIP).

    The validated competencies were from the tribes of Lubuagan, Pasil, Tinglayan, Tanudan, Dallac, Naneng, Bago and Igorot particularly Sagada. “We achieved 100% of our target” Bas-ilen said in an interview. She further said that the success is further credited to the full participation of the stakeholders.

    The IP Month Celebration was a response to call for inclusive education by the DepEd. In inclusive education, schools should also pay attention and give weight to the community competencies from the different tribes where the students came from.

    Further, the validation of the implementation of IP Education was done earlier. The validators administered a survey questionnaire and had an interview with the stakeholders. Two teachers and parents, ten students and the school head were interviewed. Questions focused on the seminars conducted about IPEd.

    The National Validation personnel Johnel Aguilera together with Division Focal Person on IPEd Emily Langkit gathered data on the needs of the IPEd implementing schools like Tabuk City National High School last August 13.

    “To fully realize IPEd in the context of inclusive education, all the learning areas should seriously participate in its implementation,” Bas-ilen concluded the interview.

    about us

    In a far-flung area of the Cordillera, lies the Province of Kalinga which cradles in its bosom the Tabuk National High School, a shining star in the North. Kalinga comprises of eight municipalities namely Tinglayan, Tanudan, Pasil, Lubuagan, Pinukpuk, Ri...zal, Balbalan and Tabuk, the latter being the commercial and educational center of the province.

    Tabuk National High School, with a total land area of 32,233 sq. meters and is formerly named Kalinga Apayao Provincial High School, is now on its 39th year of advocating quality education to 3,205 enrollees every year. It is located at the heart of Poblacion, Tabuk, Kalinga. The school was founded and headed by Mr. Augusto Alejandrino, a District Supervisor. The school was nationalized on June 1973 with Mrs. Joaquina C. Falgui as the appointed principal followed by Mrs. Purificacion E. Domingo, Mrs. Aurora E. Plan and at present by Dr. Rodolfo C. Ballog. The school has 72 teachers and 20 administrative and support staff. There are 2 building annexes: New Tanglag and Uma Lubuagan and one extension: Balong which is considered as the center of curricular and co-curricular activities.
